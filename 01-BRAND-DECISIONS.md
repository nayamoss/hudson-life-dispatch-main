## üìÖ DECISION 2: FREQUENCY

### Option A: Weekly (Tuesday) ‚≠ê RECOMMENDED
**Based on**: Catskill Crew (40K subs)

**Pros**:
- Sustainable (4-5 hours/week)
- Can do high-quality, long-form content
- Tuesday = start of week, gives readers time to plan
- Easier for solo founder

**Cons**:
- Less frequent touchpoints
- Slower audience building

**Best for**: Solo founder, quality over quantity

---

### Option B: 3x per Week (Mon/Wed/Fri)
**Based on**: Winnipeg Digest (40K+ subs)

**Pros**:
- More touchpoints = faster growth
- Can split content types:
  - Monday: Weekend recap
  - Wednesday: Weekend preview
  - Friday: Full weekend guide
- Still sustainable (10-15 hours/week)

**Cons**:
- Need content automation
- More time commitment
- Risk of burnout

**Best for**: If you have automation or team

---

### Option C: Daily (Monday-Friday)
**Based on**: Naptown Scoop (23K subs)

**Pros**:
- Maximum touchpoints
- Daily habit = high engagement
- Works for news-focused content

**Cons**:
- 20+ hours/week minimum
- Need team or full automation
- Hard to maintain quality

**Best for**: Full-time commitment or team

---

## üíª DECISION 3: PLATFORM

### Option A: Beehiiv ‚≠ê RECOMMENDED
**Used by**: Naptown Scoop, Winnipeg Digest

**Pros**:
- Built for growth (referral program, polls, boosts)
- Monetization features (ads, premium tiers)
- Beautiful templates
- Analytics dashboard
- Proven by successful newsletters

**Cons**:
- Costs $49/month after 2,500 subscribers
- Less customization than custom
- Locked into their platform

**Pricing**:
- Launch: Free (up to 2,500 subs)
- Grow: $49/month (2,500-10K subs)
- Scale: $99/month (10K-100K subs)

---

### Option B: Substack
**Used by**: Many successful newsletters

**Pros**:
- Free to start
- Built-in discovery/network
- Easy premium subscriptions (they handle billing)
- Simple, clean

**Cons**:
- 10% fee on paid subscriptions
- Less features than Beehiiv
- Harder to monetize with ads

**Best for**: If you want paid subscriptions from day 1

---

### Option C: Custom (What You Have Now)
**Used by**: Some large newsletters

**Pros**:
- Full control
- No monthly fees
- Can customize everything
- Own your data completely

**Cons**:
- Need to build everything
- No built-in growth features
- No monetization features
- More work to maintain

**Best for**: If you're technical and want full control

## üìè DECISION 4: CONTENT LENGTH

### All 4 Models Use: 12-18 Minute Reads

**Catskill Crew**: 12-16 min  
**Winnipeg Digest**: 14-17 min  
**Naptown Scoop**: 12-18 min  
**Salina311**: 1-25 min (varies by post)

### ‚úÖ RECOMMENDATION: 14-16 Minutes (2,000-2,500 words)

This is the sweet spot:
- Long enough to provide value
- Short enough to read over coffee
